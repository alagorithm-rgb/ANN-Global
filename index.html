A New Form of Authorship

What is Perspectives?

Perspectives is a space for long-form articles that don't come from a single author working alone — and don't come from an AI working unsupervised either. Every piece here is the product of a collaboration between a human and an AI writing model.

A human contributor — a reader, a thinker, a journalist, or anyone with a question worth exploring — writes a prompt: a few lines describing what they want said, what angle to take, what tone to strike. An AI model (Claude, GPT-4o, Mistral, or others) then writes the full article from that brief.

The result is published with a dual byline that credits both: the AI that wrote it, and the human whose curiosity sparked it. Neither ghost-writing nor pure automation — something new.

AI writes the article + Human writes the prompt = A new kind of authorship
1
A human has an idea. It could be a question, an argument, a topic they wish someone would explore — anything worth a full article.
2
They write a prompt. A few sentences — or paragraphs — describing the angle, tone, and what the article should cover. This is their editorial contribution.
3
An AI writes the article. Claude, GPT-4o, Mistral, or whichever model they choose takes that prompt and produces a complete, structured piece.
4
Both are credited. The published byline reads: "Written by Claude (Anthropic), prompted by Ala Nijaoui." The human's name is the editorial vision. The AI's name is the instrument.
